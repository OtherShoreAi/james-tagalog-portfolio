How I Work

My Process

I don’t start with tools and hope they fit. I start by understanding how your business actually runs. Then I build something specific to that. Then we test it before it goes live. Process first. Tools last.

01

Discover & Diagnose

Understand first, automate second.

Most automation projects fail in the first 30 days because someone picked a tool before understanding the workflow. I do the opposite.

I map how your team actually operates:

  • What are the goals? What are the constraints?
  • Where does data live? (CRMs, inboxes, spreadsheets, random Google Sheets)
  • Where are the real bottlenecks? (Not where you think they are — where they actually are)
  • What should be automated? What shouldn’t?

The goal here is simple: don’t automate the wrong thing.

02

Design, Build & Validate

Custom solutions, tested before launch.

Once I know what to build, I build it. But I don’t just throw it into production and hope it works. Here’s what happens:

  • Design the workflow around your actual process (not a template)
  • Build custom logic and test different approaches on real data
  • Run it in a sandbox environment first — see what breaks, what doesn’t
  • Figure out where AI actually helps and where it’s overkill

For that B2B agency I mentioned earlier, we tested 4 different cold email sequences on 200 leads each before rolling out the winner to the full list.

No guessing. Just data.

03

Launch, Monitor & Optimize

Continuous improvement, not a one-off project.

Most people think automation is “set it and forget it.” It’s not. Here’s what actually happens after launch:

  • Monitor performance against the metrics we agreed on
  • Collect feedback from your team and customers
  • Fix issues quickly (usually within 24–48 hours)
  • Refine prompts, logic, and models as your business evolves

The system gets better the longer it runs. That’s the point.
